# SIKEY Whitepaper v1.1  
### *Sovereign Identity Key Protocol*  
### *A Zero-Knowledge, Decentralised, Non-Correlatable Identity Framework for a Free Digital Future*

---

# **Abstract**
Digital identity is becoming the foundational layer of modern society — determining access to services, citizenship, finance, healthcare, travel, and online participation. Yet the emerging identity systems being deployed globally rely on centralised architectures that create unprecedented risks to human autonomy.

SIKEY introduces a **sovereign, decentralised, privacy-first identity protocol** built on user-owned keys, hierarchical deterministic identity derivation, selective disclosure, and zero-knowledge proofs. SIKEY allows individuals to authenticate and prove information **without revealing personal data** — preventing surveillance, correlation, and identity mining.

SIKEY is not a corporation, platform, or government initiative.  
It is a **public good**, an **open protocol**, and a **movement toward a world where identity belongs to people — not institutions.**

---

# **1. The Problem: Centralised Digital Identity Is Dangerous**

Centralised digital ID systems deployed by governments and corporations rely on:

- central databases  
- biometric harvesting  
- trackable verification flows  
- permanent identifiers  
- opaque governance  
- cross-service linkage  
- mandatory enrolment  

These systems create extreme risks:

### **1.1 Mass Surveillance**
Identity becomes a tracking mechanism for all digital and physical activity.

### **1.2 Single Point of Failure**
A single breach can compromise millions permanently.

### **1.3 Behavioural Coercion**
Access to life services becomes conditional and programmable.

### **1.4 Monopoly Control**
A single entity controls identity for every digital interaction.

### **1.5 Erosion of Human Rights**
Participation in society becomes conditional on compliance.

SIKEY provides a different path — identity that empowers, not controls.

---

# **2. The SIKEY Solution: A Sovereign Identity Key**

SIKEY — **Sovereign Identity Key** — is a cryptographic identity protocol designed around:

- **self-custody**  
- **zero-knowledge proofs**  
- **non-correlation**  
- **local credential storage**  
- **interoperability without centralisation**  

Examples:

- Prove age without revealing birthday  
- Prove licence validity without revealing address  
- Authenticate without exposing identity  
- Verify credentials without linkability  

Identity becomes **mathematical truth**, not **personal data**.

---

# **3. Principles of the SIKEY Protocol**

### **3.1 Self-Sovereignty**
Identity MUST be owned and controlled by the individual.

### **3.2 Zero-Knowledge by Default**
Verification without exposing underlying data.

### **3.3 Decentralisation & Anti-Capture**
No single authority — state or corporate — can control SIKEY.

### **3.4 Selective Disclosure**
Only the required attribute is ever revealed.

### **3.5 Non-Correlation**
Each verifier and app receives a unique derived key.

### **3.6 Open-Source & Transparent Governance**
Development must remain public, auditable, and inclusive.

# **4. SIKEY Architecture Overview**

SIKEY is composed of four layers:

---

## **4.1 User Layer — The SIKEY Wallet**
Stores:

- master SIK  
- derived context keys  
- credentials  
- proof generation logic  

Everything is local. No servers. No data retention.

---

## **4.2 Issuer Layer — Credential Attestors**
Entities such as:

- governments  
- universities  
- banks  
- employers  

Issuers **verify → issue → forget**.  
They keep **zero user data**.

---

## **4.3 Verifier Layer — Access Gateways**
Verifiers include:

- apps  
- government services  
- border authorities  
- online platforms  
- DAOs  
- web services  

They receive **zero-knowledge proofs**, not identity details.

---

## **4.4 SIKEY Registry — Trust Anchors**
A decentralised registry storing:

- issuer public keys  
- credential schemas  
- revocation commitments  

Never stores:

- PII  
- identifiers  
- logs  
- proofs  

---

# **5. Zero-Knowledge Verification Flow**

### **Step 1 — Issuance**
Issuer signs a credential and gives it to the user.

### **Step 2 — Proof Generation**
User creates a ZK proof satisfying a verifier’s requirement.

### **Step 3 — Verification**
Verifier checks:

- issuer key  
- schema  
- revocation status  

Without learning:

- who the user is  
- the rest of their credential  
- any metadata  

---

# **6. Privacy & Security Guarantees**

### **6.1 Non-Linkability**
Each context uses a unique identity key.

### **6.2 Minimal Disclosure**
Only the required data is proven.

### **6.3 Decentralised Trust**
No central authority controls identity validation.

### **6.4 Private Revocation**
Credentials can be revoked without deanonymisation.

### **6.5 User Self-Custody**
Keys are never stored by third parties.
